1 · Arrive

Character is practiced over time

In Chapter 6, you learned that ethics is not only about one isolated choice. Repeated action trains what we notice, desire, avoid, and become. This workbook helps you use the Practice Test to examine a habit route and propose a small repair practice.

2 · Read the Case

The Avoided Conversation

Case: Sam has a habit. Whenever a serious conversation begins, Sam checks their phone, makes a joke, changes the subject, or suddenly remembers something else they need to do.

Sam says, “I just do not like drama.” But over time, friends stop bringing serious concerns to Sam. They expect Sam to avoid discomfort. One friend says, “I like Sam, but I cannot trust them with hard things.”

Sam did not make one dramatic harmful choice. But Sam has practiced a route: discomfort appears, avoidance follows.

What is becoming easier?

Habits train more than behavior. They can train attention, desire, avoidance, expectation, trust, and future agency.

6 · Practical Wisdom and Supports

What does the situation call for?

Practical wisdom: the ability to judge what is called for in a concrete situation. It is not mechanical rule-following.
7 · Responsibility and Limits

Avoid shame, but do not erase responsibility

Chapter 6 should not be used to label people as permanently good or bad. Many habits form under pressure. But a habit can still be examined and repaired.
